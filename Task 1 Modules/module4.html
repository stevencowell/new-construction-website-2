Incident & Emergency Response

Knowing what to do in an emergency is critical. Every site must have a clear emergency plan.

  • First Aid: You should know who the designated First Aid Officers are and where the first aid kits and facilities are located on your site.
  • Emergency Procedures: You must be familiar with the site's emergency plan, which should be explained during your induction. This includes:
    • The signal for an emergency (e.g., a specific type of alarm or siren).
    • The location of emergency exits and paths to them.
    • The location of the emergency assembly area (or muster point).
    • How to contact emergency services (call 000 in Australia) and provide key information like the site address and nature of the emergency.
  • Responding to an Incident: If you witness an accident, your first priority is your own safety. Do not rush in. Assess the situation, raise the alarm, and only provide assistance if you are trained and it is safe to do so. Do not disturb the scene, as it may need to be investigated.
