Overview of Requirements

To be deemed competent, you must successfully complete three assessment sections covering your ability to read plans, and plan and organise work tasks.

  • Section 1: Written Questioning. This tests your knowledge of different types of construction plans, how to interpret features like title blocks and legends, and the principles of project planning.
  • Section 2: Product-Based Methods. Working in a team, you will plan a construction project by creating a Work Plan (with a JSA), a Gantt Chart for scheduling, and a detailed Materials List.
  • Section 3: Direct Observation. An assessor will observe you participating in a team planning meeting, responding to a plan inconsistency scenario, and correctly orientating a site plan to the physical site.

You must achieve a "Satisfactory" result in all parts of all three sections to be judged competent.
