Overview of Requirements

To be deemed competent, you must satisfactorily complete three major sections of assessment focusing on applying Work Health and Safety (WHS) procedures.

  • Section 1: Written Questioning. This tests your knowledge of WHS legislation, duty of care, hazard identification (including asbestos), safety documents (JSA/SDS), and emergency procedures.
  • Section 2: Product-Based Methods. You must develop a Job Safety Analysis (JSA) for practical projects, identifying hazards, assessing risks, and outlining control measures.
  • Section 3: Direct Observation. An assessor will observe you responding to safety scenarios (like finding asbestos or a fire) and applying safe work practices during your projects, including using PPE and following your JSA.

You must pass all parts of all three sections to be judged competent.
