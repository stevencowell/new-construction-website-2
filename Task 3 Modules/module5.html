5.1 What is an Emergency?

In a construction context, an emergency is any unplanned event that poses immediate danger to people, property, or the environment and requires urgent response. Common examples include fires, serious accidents, hazardous chemical spills, structural collapse, or medical emergencies.

5.2 Fire and Evacuation Procedures

  • Fire Alarms: Activate the nearest alarm and shout "Fire!" to warn others.
  • Evacuation Routes: Use marked exits and stairs, avoid lifts.
  • Assembly Point: Go to the designated area and wait for roll call.
  • Roll Call: Supervisor checks attendance, report missing persons.
  • Firefighting Equipment: If trained, use the correct extinguisher (PASS: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep).

5.3 First Aid

  • Locate First Aid Kit: Know its location and contents.
  • Contact First Aider: Call appointed first aider or dial 000 for serious cases.
  • Basic Actions: Treat minor injuries; CPR or recovery position for serious cases if trained.
  • Spinal Injuries: Do not move victims unless necessary; stabilize and wait for paramedics.
  • Report: Log all injuries in the incident book.

5.4 Chemical Spills

  • Alert Others: Warn nearby personnel and isolate ignition sources.
  • Use Spill Kit: Contain and clean using site kits if trained.
  • Evacuate if Needed: For large or toxic spills, evacuate and call emergency team.
  • Report: Inform supervisor and record spill details.

5.5 Other Emergencies

  • Vehicle/Equipment Accident: Shutdown equipment, call emergency services.
  • Structural Collapse: Evacuate, then await rescue personnel.
  • Natural Disaster: Follow site disaster plan (shelter or high ground).
  • Security Threat: Lockdown procedures and contact security/police.
