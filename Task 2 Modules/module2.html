Using Construction and Carpentry Power Tools

Units: CPCCCM2005 - Use construction tools and equipment
CPCCCA2002 - Use carpentry tools and equipment

General Power Tool Safety Principles

Power tools are indispensable on a construction site but can cause serious injury if misused. The following principles apply to all powered equipment:

  • Inspect before use - Check cords, plugs, casings and guards for damage. Never use a tool if it is defective or missing guards.
  • Use the right tool for the job - Match the tool and accessory to the material and task. Do not modify tools or use them for purposes for which they were not designed.
  • Protect against electric shock - Hand-held electric power tools and portable equipment must be protected by a residual current device (RCD) or safety switch. Examples of equipment requiring RCD protection include drills, saws, grinders and electric jackhammers.
  • Keep leads off the ground - Suspend extension leads on hooks or stands and keep them away from sharp edges, heat, water and vehicle traffic. Do not overload power boards.
  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment - Eye protection, hearing protection, gloves, dust masks or respirators and sturdy footwear should be worn as required for the specific tool and material.
  • Disconnect power before adjustments - Unplug the tool or remove the battery and allow moving parts to stop before changing blades, bits or discs or when clearing jams.
  • Secure the workpiece and maintain good posture - Clamp or support the work firmly, keep a balanced stance and ensure others are kept clear of the work area.
  • Be trained and authorised - Only trained and competent workers should operate high-risk tools such as nail guns, rotary hammer drills and jackhammers. Follow the manufacturer's instructions and any site-specific safe work method statements.

Cutting and Shaping Power Tools

Circular Saw
Used for making fast, straight cuts in timber or plywood. Ensure your timber is clamped and free from obstructions before cutting. Always keep hands clear of the blade and tie back long hair. This saw must start from the edge or a pilot hole - it can't do plunge cuts.

Sliding Compound Saw
Ideal for cross-cutting timber to length at various angles (typically between 90° and 45°). Accurate and fast. Keep fingers clear of the blade, clamp the timber securely, and use the dust extraction system where available.

Safe Usage Demonstration
Demonstrating correct body position and hand placement when using the compound slide saw. Always maintain proper stance and keep hands clear of the blade path.

Jigsaw
Used for cutting curves or detailed shapes in timber or sheet materials. Can also make straight cuts but isn't as precise. The blade is thin and flexible, so it may bend under pressure - avoid forcing it through dense material.

Reciprocating Saw
Commonly used for demolition or rough cuts through wood, metal, PVC, and nails. The blade moves back and forth. It can be hard to control for precision work but is very effective for cutting in tight spaces. Keep cords clear of the work area.

Bench Grinder
Used for sharpening metal tools like chisels and drill bits. Keep fingers well clear of the grinding wheel and always wear safety glasses and ear protection. Overheating the tool can damage the edge, so grind lightly and use water for cooling.

Router
Used for edge profiles, rebates, or slots. Select correct bit, test on scrap, move steadily clockwise around edges.

Angle Grinder
Cuts, grinds, or polishes metal and masonry using different discs. Dangerous if mishandled - check the disc is tight and guard is in place. Always wear full PPE and keep the tool away from flammable materials.

Drilling and Fastening Tools

Cordless Drill
A versatile tool for drilling holes and driving screws. Check the drill bit is secure before use. It's ideal for lighter jobs - be cautious of kickback if it jams. Always wear eye protection and don't force the drill.

Impact Driver
Powerful tool for driving large screws and bolts. Provides high torque output with minimal reaction force. Always use the correct bit and maintain proper control.

Rotary Hammer Drill
Specialized tool for drilling into concrete, masonry, and other hard materials. Combines rotation with hammer action for efficient drilling.

Nail Gun
Drives nails quickly and accurately, often used for framing or fixing. Always check the safety catch is working and never point the tool at anyone. Only fire when in contact with the work surface. Keep others clear of the line of fire.

Shaping and Surface Preparation Tools

Power Planer
Used to level framing or clean up edges. Adjust depth minimally, plane with the grain, and move tool continuously to avoid gouges.

Laminate Trimmer / Router
Used for decorative edge treatments, trenching, and shaping. Requires special bits for different tasks. Always secure your work and unplug when changing bits. Use both hands and move steadily to avoid tear-out.

Electrical Safety and Equipment

Extension Leads & RCDs
All power tools must be protected by Residual Current Devices (RCDs) or safety switches. Extension leads should be kept off the ground and away from water sources. Never overload power boards or use damaged cords.

RCD Protection
Essential electrical safety device for preventing electric shock. Must be tested regularly and used with all portable power tools on construction sites.

Air Tools and Compressed Air

Air Compressor
Provides compressed air for pneumatic tools like nail guns and air hammers. Check pressure settings and ensure proper maintenance. Always use appropriate safety equipment when working with compressed air.

Jack Hammer
Heavy-duty demolition tool for breaking hard materials like concrete and rock. Requires proper training and PPE. Always ensure the work area is clear and secure.

Key Safety Reminders

  • Always inspect tools before use - Check for damage, missing guards, or frayed cords
  • Use RCD protection - All portable power tools must be protected
  • Wear appropriate PPE - Eye protection, hearing protection, and safety footwear
  • Keep work areas clear - Remove tripping hazards and ensure good lighting
  • Follow manufacturer instructions - Read manuals and follow safe operating procedures
  • Report damaged equipment - Never use tools that are defective or unsafe
