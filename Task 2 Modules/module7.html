Tool Maintenance and Sharpening

Units: CPCCCM2005 - Use construction tools and equipment
CPCCCA2002 - Use carpentry tools and equipment

Why Maintenance Matters

Well-maintained tools work more accurately, last longer, are safer to use, and help students produce higher-quality work. Every student is responsible for inspecting, cleaning, maintaining, and correctly storing the tools they use. If a tool is damaged or faulty, it must be reported to the teacher and not used.

Maintaining and Replacing Components

  • Circular Saw - Blade Replacement: Disconnect power. Use a spanner or locking button to hold the blade. Loosen the retaining bolt, remove old blade, fit new blade with correct rotation direction, tighten and test before use.
  • Power Planer - Blade Replacement: Disconnect power. Loosen blade housing screws or release lever. Remove old blades and fit new ones evenly. Secure tightly and ensure balance across the drum.
  • Router - Bit Replacement: Disconnect power. Use two spanners or a locking button to loosen the collet. Remove old bit, insert new bit to correct depth, and tighten securely. Double-check seating.
  • Angle Grinder - Disc Replacement: Disconnect power. Lock the disc with a spanner, loosen the nut. Replace disc with correct type. Check rotation direction and tighten nut securely.

Sharpening Edge Tools (Hand Planes & Chisels)

These tools become dull over time and need regular sharpening. The sharpening process involves grinding (restoring the edge) and honing (finishing the edge).

Sharpening Equipment

Sharpening Wheel
A dedicated sharpening wheel provides consistent and precise sharpening for edge tools. Always use appropriate safety equipment and maintain proper technique to achieve optimal results.

  1. Grind the edge using a bench grinder or wet stone:
    • Chisels: grind at 30°
    • Plane blades: grind at 25°
    • Don't overheat - dip in water regularly
  2. Flatten the back of the blade on a sharpening stone:
    • Hold it completely flat
    • Slide back and forth to polish the surface
  3. Hone the bevel on a sharpening stone:
    • Set bevel flat on stone
    • Lift slightly (about 5° more than grinding angle)
    • Use consistent pressure and motion
  4. Test sharpness safely on scrap timber, not with fingers.

Router Maintenance

Router Tool
Routers require regular maintenance to ensure safe and accurate operation. Keep the collet clean, inspect bits for damage, and maintain proper depth settings for consistent results.

Router Maintenance Checklist

  • Clean the collet - Remove dust and debris regularly
  • Inspect router bits - Check for damage or wear
  • Lubricate moving parts - Follow manufacturer guidelines
  • Check depth settings - Ensure accuracy for precise cuts
  • Store properly - Keep in dry, secure location

Cleaning and Storage

  • Wipe tools clean - remove dust, glue, or shavings.
  • Apply oil - to steel surfaces if tools are not used regularly.
  • Store tools properly - return to designated racks, drawers, or shadow boards.
  • Label damaged tools - and report immediately.
  • Power tools should be unplugged or have batteries removed, with cords coiled neatly and stored off damp surfaces.

By performing these maintenance and sharpening tasks, you extend the life of your tools and ensure they are ready for use when you need them. Sharp, well-maintained tools make your work easier and produce higher-quality results. Moreover, using a dull tool can be dangerous - it often requires excessive force and can slip.

Take pride in your tools - maintenance reflects professionalism and safety.
