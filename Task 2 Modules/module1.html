Using Construction and Carpentry Hand Tools

Units: CPCCCM2005 - Use construction tools and equipment
CPCCCA2002 - Use carpentry tools and equipment

Measuring and Marking Tools

Combination Square
A multi-purpose tool used for marking 90° and 45° angles, checking depth, and finding centres. Ideal for smaller timber sections. Be cautious of pinch points when adjusting.

Retractable Tape Measure
Used to measure distances up to several metres. Always retract slowly to avoid injury from the blade snapping back. Limited to the length printed on the tape.

Builder's Square
Used for checking right angles on timber. Strong and durable but not as accurate on large, warped, or irregular materials. Can be heavy - handle carefully.

Folding or Steel Rule
Used for measuring and marking straight lines on shorter workpieces. Store flat to prevent bending. Take care with sharp edges when folding.

Spirit Level
Used to ensure horizontal (level) and vertical (plumb) surfaces. Handle with care to avoid breaking the glass vials. Less effective for long-distance levelling.

Sliding Bevel
Used to transfer and mark angles from one piece to another. Adjustable blade allows for precise angle replication. Essential for complex joinery work.

String Line
Creates a long, straight reference line between two points. Useful for laying bricks or posts. Must be kept taut and clear of foot traffic to avoid trip hazards.

Chalk Line
Used to snap a straight, visible line across a surface. Ideal for laying out framing or sheet material. Eye protection is recommended to avoid chalk dust.

Plumb Bob
Traditional tool for establishing true vertical alignment. Used when a spirit level is not practical or for very precise vertical measurements.

Water Level
Used for leveling over long distances where a spirit level is not practical. Based on the principle that water always finds its own level.

Laser Level
Modern electronic leveling tool that projects a laser beam for precise horizontal and vertical alignment. Ideal for large projects and professional use.

Straight Edge
Used to verify straight lines and check for surface irregularities. Essential for quality control and ensuring work meets specifications.

Cutting and Shaping Tools

Handsaw
Used to manually cut timber. Reliable but slower than power tools. Teeth can become blunt over time. Workpieces must be secured before use.

Coping Saw
Ideal for cutting curves in thinner timber or ply. The thin blade is flexible and suited to detail work, but care is needed to avoid snapping it.

Tenon Saw
Specialised saw for cutting tenon joints and other precision cuts. Has a stiff back for straight cuts and fine teeth for clean finishes. Essential for traditional joinery work.

Chisel
Used to shape timber, especially for joints like housings or rebates. Keep hands behind the blade and always chisel away from your body. Sharpen frequently.

Hand Plane
Used to smooth or reduce the thickness of timber. Push with the grain for best results. Must be kept sharp and flat for clean, even finishes.

Tin Snips
Used for cutting thin sheet metal. Always wear gloves and eye protection to avoid injury from sharp edges. Not suitable for thick or rigid metal.

Fixing and Assembly Tools

Claw Hammer
Used for driving or pulling nails. Grip it near the base for better control and impact. Avoid damaging surfaces when removing nails.

Wooden Mallet
Used for striking chisels and other woodworking tools without damaging them. The wooden head provides controlled force for precise work.

Rubber Mallet
Used for delicate assembly work where you need to avoid marking surfaces. Ideal for fitting joints and assembling components.

Trimming Knife
Used for cutting soft materials like insulation or plasterboard. Blades must be changed regularly. Always cut away from your body and keep fingers clear.

Clamps
Used to hold workpieces steady during cutting, drilling, or gluing. Choose the right size clamp and avoid overtightening. Position to keep work stable without blocking your cutting line.

Workholding and Support Tools

Woodworking Vice
Essential for securing workpieces during cutting, shaping, and assembly. Provides stable support and allows both hands to work safely on the project.

Metal Vice
Heavy-duty workholding tool for construction tasks. Provides secure clamping for metal work and heavy materials.

Site Work Tools

Shovel
Essential tool for digging, moving soil, and preparing foundations. Choose the right type for the specific task and maintain proper technique.

Crowbar
Used for demolition work, prying materials apart, and removing nails or fasteners. Always wear appropriate PPE when using.

Key Safety Reminders

  • Always check the tool before use - Make sure it's clean, sharp, and undamaged.
  • Use proper body positioning - Don't overreach or lean across materials.
  • Work at bench height - Avoid working on the floor to protect posture.
  • Keep your tools close and organised - Avoids unnecessary movement and distraction.
  • Communicate if unsure - Don't guess; ask for clarification during observation.
