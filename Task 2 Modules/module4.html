Module 4: Safety

Safety Practices

This module covers essential safety practices required for construction work. Understanding and implementing proper safety measures is crucial for preventing injuries and maintaining a safe work environment.

Units: CPCCCM2005, CPCCCA2002, CPCCCA2011

Planning and Preparing the Work Area

Before starting any practical task, students must conduct proper planning to ensure the site is safe, compliant, and ready for efficient work.

  • Inspect the work area - Look for trip hazards, uneven surfaces, poor lighting, or clutter.
  • Complete a Job Safety Analysis (JSA) - Identify potential hazards and outline controls.
  • Read and follow the Safe Operating Procedure (SOP) - For every tool or machine being used.
  • Review equipment manuals - Understand how to operate, adjust, and maintain tools.
  • Follow signage - Electrical hazard warnings, or restricted areas.
  • Communicate with others - Let nearby workers know when moving large items or using loud tools.

Reviewing Work Instructions

Before using tools or materials, students must review all relevant documentation to guide safe and effective work. These may include:

  • Teacher instructions (verbal or written).
  • JSAs specific to the activity.
  • SOPs for equipment and machinery.
  • Project plans or Gantt charts for timing and sequencing.
  • Equipment/machinery operator's manuals.

Students should never assume how to use a tool - review before use.

Maintaining a Clean and Safe Work Area

  • Sweep the floor regularly - Remove sawdust and offcuts immediately.
  • Place waste into correct bins - Timber in wood scrap, metal in recycling, general waste separate.
  • Keep walkways clear - Move unused tools or materials to storage zones.
  • Clean up spills immediately - Glue, paint, or water to prevent slip hazards.
  • "Clean as you go" approach to prevent hazards from accumulating.

Checking, Storing and Reporting Tools

  • Inspect for faults - Frayed cords, broken guards, cracked handles.
  • Report faults to the teacher - Do not continue using damaged equipment.
  • Clean tools - Remove dust, glue, or shavings; apply oil if needed.
  • Store tools - Return to designated racks, drawers, or shadow boards.
  • Unplug power tools or remove batteries before storage.

Hazardous Materials and Safety Data Sheets (SDS)

Be aware of all hazardous substances on site (paints, adhesives, cleaning chemicals). Know where to find the SDS for each product. The SDS provides information on safe use, handling, storage, and first aid measures. Always follow SDS guidelines and storage conditions.

Power Equipment Safety

When using power equipment on site, always follow these safety guidelines:

  • Inspect equipment before use - Check for damaged cords, missing guards, or loose parts
  • Use RCD protection - All portable power tools must be protected by Residual Current Devices
  • Wear appropriate safety gear - Eye protection, hearing protection, and sturdy footwear
  • Keep work areas clear - Remove tripping hazards and ensure good lighting
  • Follow manufacturer instructions - Read manuals and follow safe operating procedures
  • Report damaged equipment - Never use tools that are defective or unsafe
  • Maintain proper body positioning - Avoid overreaching or working in awkward positions
  • Keep cords away from hazards - Protect extension leads from water, heat, and sharp edges
  • Unplug before adjustments - Always disconnect power when changing blades, bits, or making adjustments
  • Store tools properly - Keep power tools clean and store in dry, secure locations

Emergency Procedures

Know the location of emergency equipment and procedures:

  • First aid kits and emergency contact numbers
  • Fire extinguishers and evacuation routes
  • Emergency stop buttons on machinery
  • Reporting procedures for incidents and near misses

Key Safety Principles

  • Always follow safety procedures for the task
  • Never take shortcuts with safety procedures
  • Report hazards immediately to supervisors
  • Stay alert and focused on the task at hand
  • Look out for others and communicate clearly
