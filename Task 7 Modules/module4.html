Skills Evaluation and Development Plan

Part of becoming a professional tradesperson is understanding your own strengths and weaknesses and making a plan to improve. This assessment asks you to evaluate your skills and plan for your future career.

1. Skills Evaluation

Honestly assess your current skill level for each of the listed competencies. Are you confident and can perform the task easily (Strong), or do you understand the basics but need more practice (Weak)? This self-reflection is the first step to improvement.

2. Skill Development Plan

Based on your evaluation, create a plan with actionable steps to improve. Your plan should be realistic and proactive. Examples include:

  • Seek Feedback: Actively ask your teacher for feedback on areas you've identified as weak.
  • Practise: Dedicate extra time to practising specific skills you want to improve.
  • Undertake Work Placement: Use your work placement as an opportunity to gain experience and focus on improving your weaker areas in a real-world environment.
  • Research Pathways: Look into post-school courses, apprenticeships, and what employers are looking for. Websites like the Australian Apprenticeships Pathways can provide checklists and resources.
  • Network: Talk to your Careers Adviser, local tradespeople, and employment agencies to get advice on how to become more employable.

Completing an Environmental Incident Report

If you witness an environmental incident, you have a responsibility to report it. An incident report form is a formal document used to record what happened so that it can be investigated and prevented from happening again.

How to Fill Out the Report (Based on the Scenario):

  • Details of Incident: Fill in the date, time, and exact location of the incident.
  • Description of Incident: Be clear, factual, and concise. State exactly what you saw. For example: "I observed a co-worker (Olivia Roberts) placing CCA treated pine offcuts into a fire bucket."
  • Environmental Harm Caused: Identify the potential harm. Burning CCA timber releases toxic chemicals, causing **air pollution**. It is also a waste of a potentially reusable resource, so it also relates to **resource inefficiency**.
  • Immediate Response Actions: Record the immediate actions taken to stop the incident. For example: "I immediately instructed the co-worker to stop and reported the incident to the site supervisor (Dean Johnson)."
  • Suggested Future Hazard Controls: Think about what could be done to prevent this in the future. This is about improving the system. For example: "Provide all workers with specific training on the correct handling and disposal of CCA treated timber, in line with EPA guidelines. Reinforce the workplace policy on resource efficiency and waste separation during toolbox talks."
